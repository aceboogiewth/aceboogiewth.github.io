SDLC Phase Description

Planning

Planning is deciding in advance what to do, how to do it, when to do it, and who should do it. This bridges the gap from where the organization is to where it wants to be. The planning function involves establishing goals and arranging them in logical order.

Define Requirements

A condition or capability needed by a stakeholder to solve a problem or achieve an objective.

Design & Prototyping

Prototyping is an experimental process where design teams implement ideas into tangible forms from paper to digital.

Software Development

Software development refers to a set of computer science activities dedicated to the process of creating, designing, deploying and supporting software.

Software Testing

Software testing is the process of evaluating and verifying that a software product or application does what it is supposed to do.

Deployment

The action of bringing resources into effective action.

Operations & Maintenance

Operations & Maintenance (O&M means the functions, duties and labor associated with the daily operations and normal repairs, replacement of parts and structural components,ect.

SDLC Job Role Description

planning Manager

The day-to-day role of a planning manager typically involves: Overseeing planning development plans and applications.

Business Analyst

Business analysts (BAs) are responsible for bridging the gap between IT and the business using data analytics.

UX Designer

The UX designer creates satisfying and compelling experiences for users of a product, often drawing on results from user research and workflow analysis.

Software Engineer

A Software Engineer is an IT professional who designs, develops and maintains computer software at a company.

Quality Assurance Engineer

Image result for description of Quality Assurance Engineer A QA Engineer is a professional who finds and fixes bugs in a product or program before its launch.

DevOps Engineer

A DevOps Engineer works with developers and the IT staff to oversee the code releases, combining an understanding of both engineering and coding.

Project Manager

Project managers are the driving force behind the success of every key project or program your organization seeks to accomplish.

System Analyst

Systems Analysts are high-level information technology experts who work in IT departments to guide the development of computer networks and systems.

Technical Architect

A Technical architect also referred to as an IT Systems Architect, is a systems logistics specialist who creates, maintains, and implements IT systems for a growing business or IT firm.

Front-End Developer

A front end developer has one general responsibility: to ensure that website visitors can easily interact with the page.

Automation Engineer

In IT, an automation engineer refers to someone who delivers automated solutions for software processes.

System Administrator

System administrators—also known as sysadmins—are information technology (IT) professionals who make sure an organization's computer systems are functioning and meet the needs of the organization.
